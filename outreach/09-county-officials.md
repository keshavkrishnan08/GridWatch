Plan commissions are the most underserved audience on this list — they decide
rezonings with almost no data, and the question they're actually asking is *what
did comparable counties do?* Your bans and moratoriums overlay answers that.

## Verified statutes

These five were confirmed against the statute text. **Do not cite any others
without reading them yourself first.**

| Statute | What it says |
|---|---|
| **IC 36-7-4-604** | Hearing and notice; 10-day notice to interested parties. **Each commission adopts its own hearing rules** — which is exactly why no statewide comment deadline exists. |
| **IC 36-7-4-608** | Rezoning procedure: 60-day hearing, 10-business-day certification, 90-day advisory/area vote. *This is the route used for the Prop 238 submission.* |
| **IC 36-7-4-1605** | 30-day deadline to petition for judicial review. |
| **IC 36-7-4-1613** | Defines the board's record as documents **"identified by the board as having been considered"** and used as a basis for its decision. |
| **IC 36-7-4-1614** | Grounds for review. ⚠️ *The 1614/1615 split is unverified — read both before relying on either.* |

**The practical consequence of 1613:** the record is what the body *says* it
considered. Get material date-stamped into the case file before the vote and
referenced aloud during it, or it may not be in the record at all.

## What you must find out yourself, per county

There is **no statewide written-comment deadline** — IC 36-7-4-604 delegates
hearing rules to each commission. So for every county:

1. **Call the plan commission office.** Ask how to submit written material for
   case [X], what the deadline is, and whether it will be entered into the record.
2. **Get the case number** and put it in your subject line.
3. **Ask whether it's a rezoning or a BZA matter** — they're procedurally
   different, and it changes what your submission can accomplish.

**No county contact in this file is pre-verified.** Do not mail anything to an
address you haven't confirmed yourself.
