# 09 · County commissioners and plan commissions

**Most underserved audience on the list.** They decide rezonings with almost no
data, and the question they're actually asking — *what did comparable counties
do?* — is exactly what your bans/moratoriums overlay answers.

## The legal distinction that determines everything

Under **IC 36-7-4-1016**, a **rezoning is a legislative act** and is expressly
*not* a reviewable "zoning decision." Only BZA matters (variances, special
exceptions, administrative appeals) and plat/development-plan/PUD decisions
generate a certified reviewable record.

**So:** material aimed at a *rezoning* is persuasion. Material aimed at a *BZA
special exception* is **evidence the board must answer in written findings.**
Aim for the second where you can.

## Your hooks

- **IC 36-7-4-920**: "Other persons may appear and present relevant evidence."
- **IC 36-7-4-1613** defines the record as documents "identified by the board as
  having been considered... and used as a basis for its decision." **The record
  is what the body says it considered** — so get it date-stamped into the case
  file *before* the vote and referenced aloud during it.

**Strategic note:** technical material that maps onto a *specific ordinance
criterion* — usually the "injurious to public health, safety and general welfare"
prong — must be engaged in written findings. Generic opposition need not be.
Courts remand when findings merely recite ordinance language.

**Tie your submission to the criterion by name.**

## Deadlines

There is **no statewide written-comment deadline** — IC 36-7-4-401 and -604(c)
delegate it to each commission's rules. Verified practice:

## Where it's live

11 counties with ordinances, 17 with moratoriums, 2 outright bans (Marshall,
Cass). Elkhart/Bristol APC voted 7–2 unfavorable on a ~1,000-acre hyperscale PUD
before 500+ attendees. Clinton County denied Data One/Logix, triggering the
12-month refiling bar.

## Draft

> **Subject:** Written submission — [case number], [project name]
>
> [Director],
>
> Please enter this into the case file for [case number], to be heard [date].
>
> I am the author of GridWatch Indiana, a free, nonpartisan open-source atlas of
> data centers mapped against the state's electrical grid. Every figure links to
> its source document.
>
> Relevant to the [ordinance criterion — quote it exactly]:
>
> - The proposed facility is [X] MW. For scale, that is [comparison] of [county]'s
>   current load.
> - [N] other Indiana counties have adopted ordinances addressing data centers,
>   [N] have moratoriums, and Marshall and Cass have enacted bans. [Comparable
>   county] adopted [measure] on [date].
> - The serving utility is [utility]. Data-center infrastructure filed to date in
>   its territory is [figure], sourced to [docket].
>
> I take no position on this petition. The complete dataset is public and
> auditable at [link], and I will correct any error brought to my attention.
>
> Respectfully,
> [name, address]

⚠️ Never send this with a bracket unfilled, and never include a figure you can't
point to in a document. One wrong number in a public record is worse than not
filing.
