## Why this works: they already built your thing by hand

`mirrorindy.org/data-centers/` is a **standing tracker hub** covering four sites,
with a published open callout: **"What questions do you have about data
centers?"** — collecting reader contact for follow-up reporting.

That is a literal invitation to submit exactly what you produce.

And Fenwick's *"Your AES bill might be more expensive this summer"* does **your
exact output format, manually**: 7.3% higher bills through August, ~$9.52/month
more at 1,000 kWh, sourced to AES's March IURC filing.

He is hand-computing what your calculator generates. Show him it generalizes.

## Draft

> **Subject:** The AES bill math, generalized to every Indiana utility
>
> Tyler,
>
> Your piece on AES bills this summer did the calculation I've been automating —
> 7.3%, about $9.52 more at 1,000 kWh, from the March filing.
>
> I built that for every Indiana utility. GridWatch Indiana takes a ZIP code and
> returns the serving utility, the household's current bill at their actual
> usage, and the projected data-center impact in both dollars and percent — with
> the tariff and filing behind each number. NIPSCO's model uses their real
> structure ($14.00 fixed + 20.5¢/kWh, Rate 611), and cross-checks to $151.76 at
> 672 kWh, matching NIPSCO's own stated figure.
>
> It also maps 76 tracked facilities statewide, including DC Blox on the east
> side — which came in at 52 MW after the pre-vote scale-back, not the ~80 MW
> originally pitched.
>
> Free, open-source, and every figure links to its document. Happy to have Emily
> pick apart the methodology.
>
> https://keshavkrishnan08.github.io/GridWatch/
